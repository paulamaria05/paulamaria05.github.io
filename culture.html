Feeling in Cultures

Explore how different cultures perceive and express feelings through traditions, emotions, and unique words.

Traditions and Customs

How cultures use rituals and practices to express emotions.

Hanami (Japan)

The Japanese practice of appreciating cherry blossoms represents the fleeting beauty of life and evokes feelings of serenity and gratitude.

Holi (India)

This vibrant festival celebrates joy and togetherness, where people throw colors to express happiness and mend relationships.

Untranslatable Words for Emotions

Discover unique words from around the world that capture emotions we can't translate into English.

Saudade (Portuguese)

A deep emotional state of nostalgic longing for someone or something absent.

Schadenfreude (German)

The pleasure derived from someone else's misfortune.

Sisu (Finnish)

The extraordinary determination and courage in the face of adversity.

Dor (Romanian)

A complex emotional state of longing, love, and melancholy, deeply tied to Romanian cultural identity and poetic expression.
